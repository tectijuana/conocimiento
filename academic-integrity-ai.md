## Fundamental Principles of Academic Integrity

### Core Values

Academic integrity is founded on the following principles:

1. **Honesty**: Representing your own work accurately and truthfully
2. **Trust**: Building a community where academic contributions are reliable
3. **Fairness**: Ensuring equal standards and opportunities for all students
4. **Respect**: Acknowledging the intellectual contributions of others
5. **Responsibility**: Taking ownership of your academic conduct and its consequences

### Academic Honesty Commitment

All students must:
- Submit original work that represents their own intellectual effort
- Properly attribute all sources, ideas, and assistance received
- Follow all guidelines and policies outlined in course documentation
- Report suspected violations of academic integrity
- Maintain ethical standards in all academic activities

## Definition of Academic Integrity Violations

### Plagiarism

Plagiarism is the unauthorized use or close imitation of the language, ideas, or thoughts of another person and representing them as one's own original work.

**Forms of Plagiarism**:

1. **Direct Plagiarism**: Copying text word-for-word without quotation marks and citation
2. **Self-Plagiarism**: Reusing one's own previous work without disclosure or permission
3. **Mosaic Plagiarism**: Piecing together phrases, sentences, or ideas from sources without proper attribution
4. **Accidental Plagiarism**: Failing to cite sources due to negligence or misunderstanding (still a violation)
5. **Paraphrasing without Citation**: Rewording someone else's ideas without giving credit

**Examples in Context**:
- Copying paragraphs from academic papers or technical documentation
- Using another student's research paper structure or content
- Incorporating AI-generated text without disclosure
- Reusing portions of previous coursework without explicit permission

### Fabrication and Falsification

- **Fabrication**: Creating or inventing data, sources, or citations
- **Falsification**: Manipulating or altering research data, sources, or documentation

**Examples**:
- Inventing sources or citations that do not exist
- Misrepresenting the content or conclusions of cited sources
- Altering quotations or data to support your argument
- Creating fake research results or case studies

### Unauthorized Collaboration

While collaboration during research is encouraged, the final submission must be individual work.

**Prohibited**:
- Sharing written content or code between students
- Submitting jointly written papers without explicit permission
- Having another person write substantial portions of your paper
- Copying structural elements or arguments from peers

**Permitted**:
- Discussing research topics and ideas
- Sharing references and sources
- Peer reviewing drafts and providing feedback
- Collaborative study and discussion of course material

### Multiple Submission

- Submitting the same or substantially similar work for multiple courses without disclosure and permission
- Reusing previous work from other institutions or contexts without authorization

## Artificial Intelligence Tools: Policy and Guidelines

### Acceptable Uses of AI Tools

The use of artificial intelligence tools (such as ChatGPT, Claude, GitHub Copilot, Bard, and similar technologies) is permitted under the following conditions:

#### 1. Research and Ideation Phase

**Acceptable**:
- Brainstorming research topics and questions
- Generating lists of potential sources or keywords for literature searches
- Clarifying understanding of complex technical concepts
- Exploring different perspectives on a topic
- Outlining potential paper structures

**Requirements**: No special disclosure required for this exploratory phase

#### 2. Writing Assistance

**Acceptable**:
- Grammar and spelling correction
- Style and clarity improvement suggestions
- Sentence restructuring for better readability
- Translation assistance for non-native English speakers
- Formatting and Markdown syntax help

**Requirements**: Brief acknowledgment in paper (e.g., "AI tools were used for grammar checking and writing refinement")

#### 3. Technical Assistance

**Acceptable**:
- Markdown formatting guidance
- Git and GitHub workflow clarification
- Citation format examples
- LaTeX or complex formatting assistance

**Requirements**: No disclosure required for technical tool usage

### Prohibited Uses of AI Tools

The following uses of AI tools constitute academic integrity violations:

1. **Generating Substantial Content**:
   - Using AI to write entire sections or substantial portions of the paper
   - Copy-pasting AI-generated text without significant modification and disclosure
   - Submitting AI-generated content as your own original work

2. **Analysis and Argumentation**:
   - Having AI perform the critical analysis that is the core learning objective
   - Using AI to generate conclusions or synthesize your research
   - Replacing your own intellectual effort with AI-generated insights

3. **Source Fabrication**:
   - Using AI-generated citations without verifying they exist
   - Accepting AI-provided sources without independent verification
   - Incorporating AI-generated "facts" without source validation

4. **Undisclosed Substantial Usage**:
   - Extensive use of AI assistance without proper declaration
   - Misrepresenting the extent of AI involvement in your work

## Required AI Usage Declaration

### When Declaration is Required

You must include an AI usage declaration in your paper's Acknowledgments section if you:

- Used AI to generate any text that appears in your final submission (beyond minor grammar corrections)
- Used AI to assist with research synthesis or analysis
- Used AI extensively in your research process
- Had AI help generate substantial outlines or structure

### Declaration Format

Include the following statement in your paper's Acknowledgments section:

```markdown
## Acknowledgments

### Artificial Intelligence Tools Declaration

In the preparation of this research paper, I used [specific AI tool names, e.g., ChatGPT-4, Claude, etc.] to assist with [specific tasks]. Specifically:

- [Tool name] was used for [specific purpose, e.g., "initial brainstorming of research topics"]
- [Tool name] was used for [specific purpose, e.g., "grammar and style checking"]
- [Tool name] was used for [specific purpose, e.g., "generating an initial outline that was substantially modified"]

All AI-generated content was reviewed, verified, and modified to ensure accuracy and alignment with academic standards. All sources cited in this paper were independently verified by the author. The final intellectual work and critical analysis represent my own original contribution.
```

### Declaration Examples

**Example 1 - Minimal Usage**:
```markdown
## Acknowledgments

AI grammar checking tools (Grammarly, ChatGPT) were used to improve writing clarity and correct grammatical errors. No substantive content was generated by AI.
```

**Example 2 - Moderate Usage**:
```markdown
## Acknowledgments

### Artificial Intelligence Tools Declaration

ChatGPT-4 was used during the initial research phase to brainstorm potential research topics and generate keyword lists for literature searches. The tool was also used to provide grammar and style suggestions on draft versions. All final content, analysis, and conclusions are my own original work. All sources were independently verified and evaluated.
```

**Example 3 - Substantial Usage (requiring additional justification)**:
```markdown
## Acknowledgments

### Artificial Intelligence Tools Declaration

This research made extensive use of AI tools as follows:

1. **Research Phase**: ChatGPT-4 was used to understand complex technical concepts in [specific topic] and to generate initial lists of relevant subtopics for investigation.

2. **Writing Phase**: Claude was used to generate a preliminary outline for the paper structure, which was then substantially reorganized and modified based on my research findings.

3. **Refinement Phase**: Grammarly and ChatGPT were used for grammar checking, style improvement, and clarity enhancement.

All content was independently researched, analyzed, and synthesized by the author. AI-generated suggestions were critically evaluated and modified. All sources were independently verified. The critical analysis, conclusions, and original insights represent my own intellectual contribution.
```

## Verification and Enforcement

### Detection Methods

The course employs multiple methods to ensure academic integrity:

1. **Plagiarism Detection Software**: All submissions are screened using automated plagiarism detection tools
2. **AI Detection Tools**: Submissions may be analyzed using AI content detection software
3. **Manual Review**: Instructors and TAs review submissions for consistency, voice, and originality
4. **Source Verification**: Random verification of cited sources for accuracy and existence
5. **Comparative Analysis**: Cross-comparison with peer submissions and previous course offerings

### Investigation Process

When a potential violation is identified:

1. **Initial Review**: Course instructor reviews the evidence
2. **Student Notification**: Student is notified of the concern and given opportunity to respond
3. **Meeting**: Student meets with instructor to discuss the situation
4. **Evidence Evaluation**: All evidence and student explanations are considered
5. **Decision**: Determination made regarding violation occurrence and severity

### Consequences for Violations

Consequences are proportional to the severity and intent of the violation:

#### Minor Violations (First Offense, Unintentional)
- **Examples**: Inadequate citation, accidental paraphrasing issues, incomplete AI disclosure
- **Consequences**:
  - Required revision with proper attribution
  - Grade penalty of 10-20 points
  - Mandatory academic integrity tutorial completion
  - Warning placed in course record

#### Moderate Violations
- **Examples**: Multiple citation errors, substantial undisclosed AI usage, mosaic plagiarism
- **Consequences**:
  - Zero grade on assignment
  - Cannot exceed 70% on resubmission
  - Formal academic integrity violation report
  - Possible course grade reduction

#### Severe Violations
- **Examples**: Wholesale copying, source fabrication, deliberate plagiarism, purchasing papers
- **Consequences**:
  - Zero grade on assignment (no resubmission)
  - Failing grade in course
  - Report to university academic affairs
  - Notation on academic transcript
  - Possible suspension or expulsion

#### Repeat Violations
- Automatic escalation to next severity level
- Report to university administration
- Possible permanent dismissal from program

## Best Practices for Maintaining Academic Integrity

### Research and Writing Process

1. **Take Detailed Notes**: Record sources immediately during research to avoid accidental plagiarism
2. **Use Citation Management**: Employ tools like Zotero or Mendeley to track sources
3. **Paraphrase Properly**: Substantially rewrite ideas in your own words and cite the source
4. **Quote Sparingly**: Use direct quotes only when necessary and always with citation
5. **Start Early**: Avoid time pressure that might lead to shortcuts
6. **Regular Progress**: Work incrementally rather than completing everything at once

### AI Tool Usage Guidelines

1. **Use AI as Assistant, Not Author**: AI should support, not replace, your intellectual work
2. **Verify Everything**: Never trust AI-generated facts, sources, or citations without verification
3. **Maintain Your Voice**: Ensure the final work reflects your own writing style and thinking
4. **Document Usage**: Keep records of how you used AI tools in case questions arise
5. **When in Doubt, Disclose**: If uncertain whether disclosure is needed, include it

### Citation Practices

1. **Cite Generously**: When in doubt, cite the source
2. **Direct Quotes**: Always use quotation marks and citation
3. **Paraphrased Ideas**: Cite even when using your own words to express others' ideas
4. **Common Knowledge**: Only very basic facts in your field can be considered common knowledge
5. **Multiple Sources**: Cite all sources that contributed to your understanding
