# Assessment Rubric: Research Paper Evaluation

## Overview

This document defines the assessment criteria and point distribution for evaluating student research paper submissions. The total possible score is 100 points, distributed across multiple evaluation categories that assess both technical compliance and academic quality.

---

## Grading Scale

| Score Range | Letter Grade | Description |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| 90-100 | A | Excellent - Exceeds expectations in all areas |
| 80-89 | B | Good - Meets all requirements with minor areas for improvement |
| 70-79 | C | Satisfactory - Meets minimum requirements with some deficiencies |
| 60-69 | D | Below Expectations - Significant deficiencies requiring revision |
| 0-59 | F | Unacceptable - Does not meet minimum standards |

---

## Assessment Categories and Point Distribution

### Category 1: Structural Compliance and Formatting (15 points)

| Criterion | Points | Excellent (90-100%) | Good (80-89%) | Satisfactory (70-79%) | Below Expectations (<70%) |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|---------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Directory structure and file naming | 3 | Perfect adherence to naming conventions and structure | Minor naming inconsistencies | Several structural issues | Incorrect structure |
| Front matter completeness | 2 | All metadata fields complete and accurate | 1-2 missing or incorrect fields | Several missing fields | Front matter missing or severely incomplete |
| Markdown formatting quality | 4 | Exemplary use of Markdown; proper headings, lists, tables | Generally correct with minor formatting issues | Inconsistent formatting; some structural problems | Poor formatting; difficult to read |
| Section organization | 3 | All required sections present in correct order | All sections present with minor organizational issues | Missing or misplaced sections | Severely disorganized or missing multiple sections |
| Figures and tables presentation | 3 | Professional presentation with proper captions and numbering | Minor issues with captions or numbering | Several presentation issues | Poorly presented or missing captions |

**Subtotal: 15 points**

| Research question clarity and focus | 3 | Exceptionally clear and well-focused research question | Clear research question with minor ambiguity | Somewhat unclear or unfocused question | Unclear or missing research question |

**Subtotal: 25 points**

## Additional Assessment Considerations

### Bonus Points (Optional, up to 5 points)

Exceptional submissions may earn bonus points for:
- Outstanding visual presentations or supplementary materials (+1-2 points)
- Publication-ready quality (+1-2 points)
- Significant contribution to knowledge base (+1-2 points)

Maximum final score with bonus: 105 points (recorded as 100 for grade calculation)

### Penalty Deductions

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late submission (1-7 days) | -10 points |
| Late submission (8-14 days) | -20 points |
| Late submission (>14 days) | Not accepted without prior approval |
| Incomplete submission requiring major revisions | -5 to -15 points |
| Failure to respond to reviewer feedback | -10 points |
| Academic integrity violation (minor) | -20 to -50 points + mandatory revision |
| Academic integrity violation (major) | 0 points + referral to academic affairs |
| Prohibited content (executable code, etc.) | -5 to -10 points per instance |

## Evaluation Process

### Initial Assessment

1. **Automated Checks**:
   - File structure verification
   - Word count validation
   - Markdown syntax validation
   - Plagiarism screening

2. **Reviewer Evaluation**:
   - Detailed assessment using rubric criteria
   - Inline comments for specific issues
   - Overall scoring and feedback summary

### Feedback Delivery

Students receive:
- Detailed rubric scorecard with points for each criterion
- Inline comments on specific sections requiring improvement
- Overall assessment summary
- Required revisions (if score <80 points)
- Suggestions for improvement (even for accepted papers)

### Revision and Resubmission

- **First Revision**: Students may revise and resubmit if initial score is 70-79 points
- **Second Revision**: Additional revision allowed if significant effort shown and score improves
- **Maximum Score After Revision**: Capped at 85 points unless extraordinary circumstances
- **Revision Deadline**: Must be completed within 10 days of feedback receipt

---

## Grading Transparency and Appeals

### Score Justification

All assessments must include:
- Point-by-point breakdown using this rubric
- Specific examples or references to support scores
- Clear explanation for any penalty deductions
- Constructive feedback for improvement

### Appeal Process

Students may appeal their grade if they believe:
1. Rubric was not applied consistently
2. Specific criteria were misunderstood or misapplied
3. Significant errors occurred in evaluation

**Appeal Procedure**:
1. Submit written appeal within 5 days of grade receipt
2. Provide specific rubric items in question with justification
3. Include reference to relevant submission content
4. Appeal reviewed by course instructor or committee
5. Final decision communicated within 7 days

---

## Rubric Application Guidelines for Reviewers

### Consistency Standards

- Apply rubric uniformly across all submissions
- Provide specific examples when deducting points
- Focus on substantive issues, not minor stylistic preferences
- Consider context and topic complexity in evaluation

### Constructive Feedback

- Balance critical assessment with recognition of strengths
- Provide actionable suggestions for improvement
- Reference specific sections or examples
- Encourage continued learning and development

### Ethical Considerations

- Maintain objectivity and fairness
- Respect student effort and intellectual contribution
- Protect confidentiality of submissions and scores
- Report suspected academic integrity violations appropriately

---

## Continuous Improvement

This rubric is subject to periodic review based on:
- Student performance patterns and feedback
- Alignment with course learning outcomes
- Changes in academic standards or requirements
- Recommendations from accreditation reviews

Students and reviewers are encouraged to provide feedback on the clarity and fairness of assessment criteria for future rubric refinement.
